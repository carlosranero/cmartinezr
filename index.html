About Me

I am a Professor of Mathematics at University of Mathematics, specializing in algebraic topology and differential geometry. My research focuses on the intersection of these fields with theoretical physics, particularly in the areas of quantum field theory and string theory.

I received my Ph.D. from Princeton University in 2008 under the supervision of Dr. Michael Atiyah. Before joining UM, I was a postdoctoral researcher at the Institute for Advanced Study and held a visiting position at Cambridge University.

My recent work explores the homotopy theory of derived algebraic geometry, with applications to quantum mechanics. For example, the derived moduli stack of perfect complexes can be represented as:

\[ \mathcal{M}_{\text{perf}} \simeq \coprod_{n\in\mathbb{Z}} \mathcal{M}^{[n]}_{\text{perf}} \]

Research

My research program explores the deep connections between algebraic topology and theoretical physics. I'm particularly interested in how methods from homotopy theory can be applied to understand the mathematical structures underlying quantum field theories.

Recent work has focused on extending the Atiyah-Singer index theorem to non-commutative geometries:

\[ \text{Ind}(D) = \int_{T^*M} \hat{A}(TM) \wedge \text{ch}(\sigma(D)) \]

My current work focuses on developing a comprehensive framework for understanding topological phases of matter using techniques from higher category theory and homotopy theory. This research has potential applications in quantum computing and condensed matter physics.

I collaborate with physicists and mathematicians worldwide, and my work has been supported by grants from the National Science Foundation and the Simons Foundation.

Teaching

MATH 501: Advanced Algebraic Topology

Graduate course covering fundamental concepts in algebraic topology, including homotopy theory, spectral sequences, and category theory.

Covers fundamental groups, covering spaces, and higher homotopy groups:

\[ \pi_n(X,x_0) \cong [S^n, X] \]

MATH 420: Differential Geometry

Undergraduate course on differentiable manifolds, Riemannian geometry, and applications to physics.

Introduces curvature tensors and the Einstein field equations:

\[ R_{\mu\nu} - \frac{1}{2}Rg_{\mu\nu} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^4}T_{\mu\nu} \]

MATH 650: Seminar in Topological Quantum Field Theory

Graduate seminar exploring recent developments in TQFT and related areas.

MATH 310: Introduction to Topology

Undergraduate introduction to point-set topology and basic algebraic topology.
